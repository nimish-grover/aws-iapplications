Water conservation structures are essential for rainwater harvesting, groundwater recharge, and drought resilience. They support rural livelihoods and enhance ecological sustainability.

Check Dams

Small barriers built across streams to slow water flow, recharge groundwater, and reduce erosion.

Farm Ponds

Excavated ponds that collect surface runoff to support irrigation and livestock in rural farms.

Percolation Tanks

Large earthen tanks that allow water to slowly seep into the ground and recharge aquifers.

Contour Trenches

Shallow ditches dug along slopes to capture rainwater and prevent soil erosion.

Rooftop Rainwater Harvesting

Collecting and storing rainwater from roofs to supplement household water needs and recharge wells.
